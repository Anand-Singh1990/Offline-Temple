"Whenever dharma declines and adharma rises, I manifest myself." - Bhagavad Gita 4.7

In this age of constant connectivity, we have forgotten the ancient wisdom of silence. The Vedas teach us that true knowledge comes not from endless consumption of information, but from turning inward - from the practice of pratyahara, the withdrawal of senses.

When the mind is constantly fed with stimuli, it becomes like a turbulent ocean. But when we embrace stillness, when we disconnect from the external world, the waters become calm. In this calmness, we can see our true reflection - our Atman, the eternal self.

"Yoga is the cessation of the fluctuations of the mind." - Patanjali's Yoga Sutras 1.2

The great rishis sought enlightenment not in the noise of the world, but in the silence of forests and mountains. They understood that wisdom emerges from the gaps between thoughts, from the space that opens when we are not constantly distracted.

Being offline is not a limitation - it is vairagya, a conscious detachment from worldly entanglements. It is in these moments of digital tapasya that we can reconnect with something deeper, something eternal that exists beyond the realm of notifications and updates.
